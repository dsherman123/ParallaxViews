About

About Parallax Views

Parallax Views, LLC is led by David S. Sherman, Ph.D.—entrepreneur, business strategist, educator, licensed psychotherapist, and Co-Founder of Criteria Corp.
David combines psychology, technology, and product design with proven real-world success to help founders and organizations make smarter, faster, evidence-based decisions. With his diverse background, he applies parallax thinking—examining ideas from multiple, often contrasting perspectives—to uncover blind spots, challenge assumptions, and build stronger strategies.

Entrepreneurial Journey

In the early 1980s, David was Principal and Co-President of AIMS Media, where he and his brothers modernized a vast 16mm film and filmstrip library into AIMS Multimedia, a pioneer in educational technology. Drawing on Pinchot’s principles of intrapreneuring, he spearheaded the development of DigitalCurriculum, the first streaming video-on-demand service for education, which ultimately served more than 20 million students before being acquired by Discovery Communications, Inc. in 2004.

After a brief retirement, David co-founded Criteria Corp, the first SaaS pre-employment assessment platform, serving as COO and CFO until 2024. Under his leadership, Criteria grew into a global talent assessment and development platform—delivering validated pre- and post-hire assessments, structured interviewing solutions, and collaboration tools. Today, Criteria serves over 4,000 organizations worldwide, with more than 60 million assessments administered in 30 languages across 60+ countries. David continues to serve on its Board of Directors.

Beyond Business

Alongside his entrepreneurial work, David remains deeply engaged in psychology and the arts. He is a licensed psychotherapist and is currently completing his debut novel, A Question of Balance—a psychological-philosophical murder mystery.

Parallax Thinking Analysis and Considerations.

 1. Founder's Perspective

  • Vision, values, and personal goals
  • Risk tolerance and leadership style
  • Strengths and blind spots

 2. Customer Perspective

  • Needs, pain points, and desires
  • Emotional and rational buying triggers
  • User experience across touchpoints

 3. Market & Competitor Perspective

  • Industry trends and disruptions
  • Competitive positioning and differentiation
  • Market gaps and underserved segments

 4. Financial Perspective

  • Revenue models and pricing strategies
  • Cash flow, burn rate, and funding needs
  • Investor expectations and ROI

 5. Operational Perspective

  • Scalability of systems and processes
  • Supply chain and resource management
  • Team structure and productivity

 6. Team & Culture Perspective

  • Hiring strategy and talent development
  • Internal communication and collaboration
  • Company culture and values alignment

 7. Strategic Perspective

  • Short-term vs. long-term trade-offs
  • Strategic partnerships and alliances
  • Exit strategies and growth pathways

 8. Societal & Ethical Perspective

  • Social impact and sustainability
  • Ethical implications of business decisions
  • Regulatory and legal compliance

 9. Contrarian or “Devil’s Advocate” View

  • What if the core assumptions are wrong?
  • What would a critic or competitor say?
  • What happens if the market shifts suddenly?
