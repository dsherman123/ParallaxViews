About

About Parallax Views

Parallax Views, LLC is led by David S. Sherman, Ph.D.—entrepreneur, business strategist, educator, and licensed psychotherapist. With a career spanning technology, media, psychology, and product design, David brings a unique perspective to strategy and leadership. At the core of his work is parallax thinking—the practice of examining ideas from multiple, often contrasting viewpoints to uncover blind spots, challenge assumptions, and create deeper insight. This philosophy shapes the mission of Parallax Views: helping founders and organizations make smarter, faster, evidence-based decisions.

Entrepreneurial Journey

In the early 1980s, David was Principal and Co-President of AIMS Media with his brothers, where he modernized its 16mm film and filmstrip library into AIMS Multimedia, a pioneer in educational technology. Drawing on Pinchot’s principles of intrapreneuring, he spearheaded the development of DigitalCurriculum, the first streaming video-on-demand service for education, which ultimately served more than 20 million students before being acquired by Discovery Communications, Inc. in 2004.

After a brief retirement, David co-founded Criteria Corp, the first SaaS pre-employment assessment platform, where he served as COO until his second retirement in 2024. Under his leadership, Criteria expanded into a global talent assessment and development platform—delivering validated pre- and post-hire assessments, structured interviewing solutions, and collaboration tools. Today, Criteria supports more than 4,000 organizations worldwide, with over 60 million assessments administered in 30 languages across 60+ countries. David continues to serve on its Board of Directors.

Beyond Business

Alongside his entrepreneurial work, David remains deeply engaged in psychology and the arts. He is a licensed psychotherapist and continues to advise startups and young entrepreneurs. In addition, he has dedicated himself to his literary work, most notably his debut novel, A Question of Balance —a psychological-philosophical murder mystery.
